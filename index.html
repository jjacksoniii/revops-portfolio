Point of View

The AI-Native RevOps Stack Is Coming — Most Teams Aren't Ready

The RevOps stack of 2027 won't be a better version of today's stack. It'll be a fundamentally different architecture. The current model — CRM as system of record, BI layer on top, point solutions duct-taped together — was designed for a world where data was scarce and human judgment was the bottleneck. That world is ending.

AI-native RevOps means three things: predictive pipeline management that replaces gut-feel forecasting, autonomous data hygiene that eliminates the "garbage in" problem at the source, and intelligent workflow orchestration that routes the right action to the right person at the right time without manual rules engines.

The RevOps leaders who thrive will be the ones who can architect this transition — not by ripping and replacing overnight, but by building an AI layer on top of existing infrastructure that compounds in value as data quality improves. That's the design problem I'm most interested in solving.
