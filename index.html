Point of View
The AI-Native RevTech Stack

Most Revenue Teams Are Building on Architecture That's About to Become Obsolete

The RevTech stack of 2028 won't be a better version of today's stack. It will be a fundamentally different architecture. The current model (CRM as system of record, BI layer on top, point solutions stitched together with manual handoffs) was designed for a world where data was scarce and human judgment was the bottleneck. That world is ending.

AI-native RevOps changes three things at once. First, pipeline management shifts from gut-feel forecasting to signal-based conviction. Second, data hygiene becomes autonomous, eliminating the garbage-in problem at the source instead of compensating downstream. Third, workflow orchestration becomes intelligent: routing the right intervention to the right person at the right time, without a rules engine that breaks every quarter.

The companies that win will be the ones that redesign their operating model before layering AI on top. After building RevOps functions at Canva, Snowflake, and DocuSign, the pattern is clear: most revenue teams can't take advantage of AI because their data is too dirty, their processes too inconsistent, and their forecasting too dependent on human override. AI compounds in value only when the foundation is clean. That's the design problem worth solving first, and it's where I focus my advisory work.
